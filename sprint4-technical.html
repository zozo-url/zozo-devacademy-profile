SPRINT FOUR TECHNICAL BLOG

Tell your non-tech friend a story about a time you:

Got blocked on a simple problem.

I needed to access a value from an object (which is a block of code that has particular properties you define), and the line of code you use to do that includes dots and/or brackets to follow the route of access, kinda like going through the series of folders on your computer to get to the file you want. I couldn’t get my head around when to use dots or brackets and when I tried a few different combinations I couldn’t figure out what I was doing wrong! So I read through the article that was linked in the kata exercise about it, and cross-referenced this with a few other websites. I felt like I understood the concept but the thing still wasn’t working. In the end, I kept trying things and looking at example code, then I realised I had quotation marks around the code I’d put in brackets, which I didn’t need to do. I was very frustrated initially because I thought I was doing what logically made sense based on what I was reading, but still getting confused. I learnt that it’s important to break apart what you’ve written so you can notice small errors.

Solved a problem in an elegant way

I started an exercise by laying out the pseudocode (lines of text that are just step by step descriptions of what you want the code to do). I did this one step at a time, fulfilling each part to pass each smaller test. I was able to complete the whole thing relatively quickly, and when I came across issues I had a clear understanding of what I was trying to do with the code so I could come up with solutions more efficiently. I felt calm and focused during the process and learnt that I was very capable of completing the tests if I kept myself focused.

Reflect on how confident you feel using the problem solving techniques and process:

Having now applied multiple problem solving techniques, I am starting to figure out what works for me and feel confident in my capacity to reach a solution. Because I still need time for certain concepts to sink it, it was hard to initially extract benefit from things such as the rubber ducky method or pseudocode. I found that I couldn’t really reach the solutions through my own thinking alone at this point, and so relied heavily on google for support. I feel that I now know good resources for learning certain things and how to effectively cross reference different types of resources in order to reach understanding. After educating myself further, I could then apply some other methods such as reading error messages and console logging to get myself to a solution.
